## Register with GitLab

To manage the version of your canvas in Cloud Studio, integration with a Version Control System (VCS) is required.<br/>
Additionally, VCS account integration must precede the linking of a Terraform Cloud account for project validation and deployment.<br/>
This page explains how to register your GitLab account as a VCS.

<br />

<details>
    <summary>Gitlab ?</summary>
    <h5>
       GitLab is a platform for hosting and managing software development projects.
    </h5>
</details>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

1. [Gitlab](https://gitlab.com/) After signing up and logging into GitLab, click on the `Profile Icon - Preferences`in the top left corner.

<img width="100%" src={gitlab01} alt="otp img" />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

2. In the left sidebar, click on `Access tokens`, then click the `Add new token` button on the right.

<img width="100%" src={gitlab02} alt="otp img" />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

3. Enter the `Token Name` and `Token Expiration`, then select the permissions for the GitLab token in the Select scopes options by choosing `api`, `read_repository`, and `write_repository`. Finally, click the `Create personal access token` button to generate the token.

<img width="100%" src={gitlab03} alt="otp img" />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

4. Verify the issued token.

<img width="100%" src={gitlab04} alt="otp img" />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

:::caution

The issued token cannot be verified again, so make sure to save it separately.

:::

<p>&nbsp;</p>

5. In Cloud Studio, click on `My Information - Plugin Management`, then click the `+` button to link your VCS account.

<img width="100%" src={github05} alt="otp img" />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

6. Select `GitLab` as the VCS, enter the `Username` of your GitLab account and the issued `Token`, then click the `Register` button.

<img width="100%" src={gitlab05} alt="otp img" />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

7. Once you have entered a valid GitLab account `Username` and the issued `Token`, the GitLab integration will be successfully completed.

<img width="100%" src={gitlab06} alt="otp img" />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

:::caution

To integrate with GitLab Community and GitLab Enterprise versions, additional inputs for the hosted `HTTP URL` and `API URL` are required.

:::
